Risk Management

Risk Management assists in removing and/or reducing any health and safety risks within your workplace. It is thinking about what could go wrong at your workplace and what the consequences could be.

Steps

Risk Management is the 4-step process:

  1. Identify Hazards
  2. Assess Risks
  3. Control Risks
  4. Review Control Measures

There are four arrows in the diagram. Click the one that says Identify.

Step 1: Identify Hazards

If you identify a potential hazard that could cause harm in the workplace you must report it. You can report the hazard by:

  • completing an online indicent/near-miss/hazard report found on the Neo HSE (Australia) site
  • advising your manager, or
  • advising a HSE committee member.

Potential hazards are also identified through worksite inspections conducted regularly at your worksite.

Now click the arrow that says Assess.

Step 2: Assess Risks

Once a hazard has been identified, the risk must then be assessed. By assessing the risk we can determine:

  • the consequence or harm that may occur, and
  • the likelihood of this harm occurring.

From this assessment a risk rating is determined as:

  • extreme
  • high
  • moderate, or
  • low

The risk category is then used to determine the urgency and process of action required and the level of management notification for action required.

Now click the one that says Control.

Step 3: Control Risks

Risk and hazard controls involve identifying and implementing the most effective control measure that is reasonably practicable in the circumstances.

The Hierarchy of Risk Control is used to determine the most effective control. You will learn more about the Hierarchy of Risk Control later in this module.

Lastly, the one that says Review.

Step 4: Review Control Measures

Once you have identified, assessed and controlled the risk, you should review it again to ensure the work environment is safe.

Controls are reviewed to:

  • Monitor the adequacy of the proposed controls.
  • Ensure controls have been implemented.
  • Determine that no additional hazards have been created.
  • Ensure the controls have had the effect intended.
