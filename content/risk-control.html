Risk Management — Hierarchy of Controls

The hierarchy of controls provides you with a ranking of options that help you to eliminate or lower risks associated with workplace hazards. You should always select the highest possible level. The highest level of control is to eliminate the risk. If safety is our target we can reduce risks by using one of these controls.

1. Eliminate

This is the most effective control. Eliminate the risk by completely removing it or changing the work processs—for example, removing chemicals onsite by cleaning the floors without chemicals.

If Elimination is not possible, consider Substitution.

2. Substitute

Replace the hazard with something less dangerous—for example, replacing an old chair with an adjustable chair suited to the individual and their specific requirements.

If Substitution is not possible, use Engineering Control.

3. Engineering Control

These controls change the physical characteristics of the workplace or equipment. This may involve redesigning the task, equipment or mechanical device—for example, implementing ventilation systems or non-slip material used on walkways.

If Engineering Controls are not possible, consider Administrative Control.

4. Administrative Control

Use policies, procedures, signs and training as ways of controlling the risk—for example, H&S inductions, H&S training and signage.

It may be possible that Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) is also relevant.

5. Personal Protective Equipment

Personal Protective Equipment or PPE provides protection against the hazard for example, safety vests, gloves, facial masks or a fall arrest harness. This may be used in conjunction with any of the previous controls.
